Passage of Time

Alexandra Bradley

inspired by Sappho's 147

Note: This poem is written by Alexandra Bradley, who was inspired in presenting a meaning to Sappho's peoms, from which only phrases or words survived the test of time. Those words were italicized throughout the peom.

Someone will remember us, after we are gone.
We are people who live in the past,
but we are famous in our own right.
We are written down in poetry, placed in books,
Painted in pictures, and told stories about by descendants.

Perhaps our names will be forgotten, but who we are,
I say, is something that will live through the ages.
Perhaps it will be the other way around,
where only our names are remembered.

Perhaps, like the gods, we may one day not be remembered
Because we became unnecessary in the future.
But even then, someone someday, will uncover our lives
And we will live on,through myths and legends,
Through memory and tales, even in another time.
